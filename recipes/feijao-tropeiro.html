Feijão Tropeiro

Description

The feijão tropeiro is from the time of Brazil Colony, when the tropeiros, those men who walked with their troops, guiding animals, took goods throughout the country. Their diet consisted basically of beans, bacon, flour, black pepper, cornmeal and Coité (a kind of vinegar sauce with some squeezed fruits). In their stops, they ate the beans almost without broth, sun meat with a farofa and chopped cabbage. And this food, which at the time was constant, even for lack of option, became our famous common bean, taking the name of its main tasters, which until today is successful and became even better.

Ingredients

  • 150 g of chopped bacon
  • 250 g chopped sausage calabresa
  • 1 cup (coffee) olive oil
  • 2 onions chopped
  • 6 cloves of garlic crushed
  • 500 g cooked and drained beans
  • 1 cube of bacon broth
  • 4 fried eggs whole and chopped
  • smell-green to taste
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon raw cassava flour
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Put the olive oil in a pan and fry the bacon and sausage separately, then set aside.
  2. In the same pan, fry the onion and garlic, add the drained beans and the bacon broth.
  3. Add again the bacon and sausage, add cassava flour and fried eggs chopped.
  4. Add plenty of parsley
  5. Hit or salt and pepper is ready to serve.
